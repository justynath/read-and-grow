Current reading

We are currently reading "The Psychology of Money" by Morgan Housel. The book delves into the intricate interplay between human behavior and financial decisions. Through engaging narratives and practical insights, the book explores how our emotions, biases, and experiences shape our relationship with money, offering valuable wisdom for achieving financial stability and success.

Discussion Guide

  1. What were your key takeaways from the book?
  2. Do you agree that financial success is more about behavior than intelligence?
  3. How would you describe your relationship with money, and how has it changed over time?
  4. "Getting wealthy slowly" versus "getting rich quickly": Which approach resonates more with you?
  5. How do you differentiate between luck and skill in your financial journey?
  6. How do you incorporate the concept of "room for error" into your financial decisions?
  7. How can individuals prepare for unexpected financial events?
  8. Which psychological biases do you struggle with, and how do you overcome them?
  9. How do you maintain a long-term perspective in your investing approach?
  10. What does "having enough" mean to you, and how does it influence your financial goals?
